Albert Einstein

A pillar of modern physics

A timeline of Albert Einstein most notable achievements:

  • 1879: Born on March 14 in Ulm, Germany.
  • 1900: Graduated from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich with a teaching diploma in mathematics and physics.
  • 1905: Published four groundbreaking papers in "Annalen der Physik".
  • 1909: Appointed associate professor at the University of Zurich.
  • 1911: Became a full professor at the German University in Prague. Predicted the bending of light by gravity, later confirmed during a solar eclipse.
  • 1913: Returned to Germany as a professor at the University of Berlin and became a member of the Prussian Academy of Sciences.
  • 1915: Published the Theory of Relativity, redefining gravity as the curvature of spacetime.
  • 1919: Theory confirmed during a solar eclipse, making Einstein an international celebrity.
  • 1921: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for the photoelectric effect, not for relativity.
  • 1922: Began worldwide lecture tours, spreading his ideas globally.
  • 1933: Fled Nazi Germany and settled in Princeton, New Jersey, working at the Institute for Advanced Study.
  • 1939: Co-signed a letter to U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt, warning about Nazi Germanyâ€™s potential nuclear weapons development, influencing the creation of the Manhattan Project.
  • 1945: Advocated against the use of nuclear weapons following their deployment in Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
  • 1950: Published the "Unified Field Theory," attempting to unify the forces of nature, though it was incomplete.
  • 1955: Died on April 18 in Princeton, New Jersey, leaving behind a monumental scientific legacy.
  • 2013: shares the Nobel Prize in Physics with Francois Englebert "for the theoretical discovery of a mechanism that contributes to our understanding of the origin of mass of subatomic particles".
